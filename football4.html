Zinedine Zidane, born June 23, 1972, is a retired French professional footballer and current manager, widely regarded as one of the greatest players in history. He played as an attacking midfielder for Cannes, Bordeaux, Juventus, and Real Madrid, and is celebrated for his elegance, vision, ball control, and technique. Zidane led France to victory in the 1998 FIFA World Cup and the 2000 European Championship. He also won numerous titles with his clubs, including the UEFA Champions League with Real Madrid in 2002. Playing Career: Clubs: Zidane played for Cannes, Bordeaux, Juventus, and Real Madrid. International: He was a key player for France, helping them win the 1998 FIFA World Cup and the 2000 European Championship. Key Achievements: Zidane won two Serie A titles with Juventus, a La Liga title and the UEFA Champions League with Real Madrid. Awards: He was named FIFA World Player of the Year three times and won the Ballon d'Or in 1998. Retirement: Zidane retired after the 2006 World Cup, where he was awarded the Golden Ball despite his ejection in the final. Managerial Career: Zidane has also had a successful managerial career, primarily with Real Madrid. He has won three Champions League titles, two Club World Cups, two European Super Cups, two La Liga titles, and two Spanish Super Cups with Real Madrid. Playing Style and Legacy: Zidane was known for his exceptional dribbling skills, vision, and leadership. He is considered one of the most elegant and technically gifted players of all time. His performances in the 1998 World Cup final and the 2002 Champions League final are particularly memorable. Despite his controversial ejection in the 2006 World Cup final, he remains a revered figure in football history.
